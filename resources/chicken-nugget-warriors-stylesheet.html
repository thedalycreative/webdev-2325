Vibe & usage

Overall tone

Whimsical, friendly, snack-sized heroic. Think Saturday morning cartoons, internet meme energy, and fast-food mascots with a dash of kawaii.

Best use cases

  • Student web design projects
  • UI exercises & component demos
  • Mini-comics & panels
  • Reaction images & memes
  • Social media mockups

Typography

Fonts should feel bold, friendly, and readable. Avoid super-thin or overly formal typefaces.

Primary display font

Font: Luckiest Guy (Google Fonts) – for titles, character names, big headings.

Illustration style rules

Linework

Use thick, clean outlines with slight rounded corners. Avoid sketchy or overly textured lines for main characters.

Shapes & proportions

  • Big eyes, clear expressions
  • Short limbs, simple hands
  • Rounded corners, no sharp edges
  • Exaggerated eyebrows and mouths

Motion & posing

Characters should feel mid-action: waving, jumping, reacting. Avoid stiff “passport photo” poses where they just stand straight with no personality.

Backgrounds

Keep backgrounds soft and light (beige, cream) with minimal detail so the characters remain the focus. Simple shadows under characters are enough.
